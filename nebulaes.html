Nebulaes

In the universe, there are five different catagories of nebulae! These are:

  • Nebulae:

    • - Named emission nebulae because they emit their own light

    - Form when the intense radiation of stars near the nebula energizes gas

    - A star's ultraviolet radiation can flood the cloud of gas with so much energy that it can strip electrons from the nebula's hydrogen atoms (ionization)

    - Orion nebulae

  • Reflection Nebulae:

    • - Reflect the light from stars nearby

    - The stars light scatter throughout the gas and dust, causing it to glow

    - These nebulae are often blue in color

    - NGC 1999

  • Dark Nebulae:

    • - Also known as absorption nebulae

    - Clouds of gas and dust that don't emit or reflect light but block light from behind them

    - Contains large amounts of dust which allows these nebulae to absorb visible light

    - Earned the name “a hole in the sky”

    - Among these nebulae are Bok globules which are small cold clouds of gas and dense cosmic dust

  • Planetary Nebulae:

    • - Some of the fuzzy object reassembled planets

    - Form during the death of the low-mass to medium-mass stars

    - When a star casts off its outer layers, it can become rings, hourglasses, rectangles, and more

    - As the star casts off outer layers, it leaves behind its core which becomes the white dwarf star. The white dwarf stars ultraviolet radiation ionizes the gas of the planetary nebulae and causes the glow

    - The sun is expected to form a planetary nebulae at the end of its life.

  • Supernova Remnants:

    • - Some stars explode into a supernova and their contents get flung into space at 9,000-25,000 miles/second

    - When a star has lots of mass (at least 5x the sun) or in which the white dwarf star can gravitationally pull mass from a companion star, it can explode with a brightness of 10 billion suns

    - Made up of material from exploded stars and interstellar material
